The bill defines ânatural reductionâ as the 5 contained, accelerated conversion of human remains to soil. 6 The bill prohibits a person other than a funeral director 7 from providing natural reduction services and requires that 8 natural reduction services be provided in a natural reduction 9 establishment, as defined in the bill. The bill requires the 10 board of mortuary science to adopt rules for the provision 11 of natural reduction services and the licensure of natural 12 reduction establishments, which shall include a minimum setback 13 distance of 500 feet from any drinking water source. To 14 qualify for a natural reduction establishment license, the bill 15 requires a person to submit an application and license fee as 16 determined by the board. 17 The bill requires the commissioner of insurance 18 (commissioner) to adopt rules for the interment of naturally 19 reduced human remains at a cemetery and prohibits a cemetery 20 from interring naturally reduced human remains unless it 21 complies with the rules of the commissioner. The bill directs 22 the commissioner to inform the board of mortuary science of all 23 cemeteries that can receive naturally reduced human remains. 24 By operation of law, a person who violates a provision 25 of the bill may be subject to a civil penalty as determined 26 by the board and a person who makes a false statement in an 27 application for a natural reduction establishment license 28 is subject to all penalties prescribed for making a false 29 affidavit.

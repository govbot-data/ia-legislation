A provisional coaching authorization expires upon 9 the earlier of the end of the sport season for which the 10 individual was issued the authorization or the passage of 180 11 days from the issuance of the authorization. 12 The bill requires the BOEE to allow an individual holding a 13 provisional coaching authorization to convert the authorization 14 to a coaching authorization if the individual successfully 15 completes the preexisting requirements to obtain a coaching 16 authorization; successfully completes a coaching education 17 course provided by an area education agency; successfully 18 completes the coaching credential stack created by NFHS; 19 successfully completes relevant higher-education coursework; 20 provides the BOEE with evidence of prior coaching experience; 21 or if the individual successfully completes comparable 22 leadership and safety training. 23 Current law authorizes a school district to employ for 24 head coach of any interscholastic athletic activities, or for 25 assistant coach of any interscholastic athletic activity, an 26 individual who possesses a coaching authorization issued by 27 the BOEE or a teaching license with a coaching endorsement. 28 Current law also establishes the order in which school 29 districts are required to consider applicants for such 30 positions, with individuals who possess a valid teaching 31 license with a proper coaching endorsement being considered 32 first, individuals who possess a coaching authorization being 33 considered second, and individuals with a transitional coaching 34 authorization being considered third.

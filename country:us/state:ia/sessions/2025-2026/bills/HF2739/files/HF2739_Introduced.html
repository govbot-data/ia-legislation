By striking the taxation of HMO premiums in Code 1 chapter 432 and creating the new health care-related tax in 2 Code chapter 432B, the tax credits available to an HMO in Code 3 chapter 432 are not available to an HMO in new Code chapter 4 432B. 5 The bill applies the health care-related tax on the 6 taxable funds of an HMO. The bill 13 excludes from the definition of taxable funds the payments made 14 to an HMO by the United States secretary of health and human 15 services under a contract and pursuant to federal authority. 16 If a payment qualifies as both a payment received by an HMO for 17 services provided and a payment made by the HMO to providers, 18 the bill requires the HMO to classify the payment as a payment 19 received by the HMO. 20 Currently, the tax rate on premiums in Code section 432.1(2) 21 is gradually being reduced and the current rate for calendar 22 year 2026 is .925 percent of premiums. The new tax rates on 23 the taxable funds of an HMO in the bill are as follows: for 24 the period in calendar year 2026 beginning January 1, 2026, 25 and ending September 30, 2026, 3.5 percent; for the period 26 in calendar year 2026 beginning on or after October 1, 2026, 27 and ending December 31, 2026, .95 percent; and for the 2027 28 calendar year and subsequent calendar years, the rate remains 29 at .95 percent. 30 Currently, the premium tax is due March 1 of the year 31 following the calendar year for which the tax is due; however, 32 one-half of the premium taxes are required to be prepaid on 33 June 1 and one-half on August 15.

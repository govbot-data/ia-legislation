Both Code chapters 459 7 and 459A regulate the application of manure and other effluent 8 originating from an operation, including application close 9 to water sources. AFOs are also regulated under federal law 10 by the United States environmental protection agency, which 11 administers and enforces the national pollutant discharge 12 elimination system program (NPDES program) pursuant to the 13 federal Water Pollution Control Act of 1972 (33 U.S.C. ch. 26, 14 as amended, and 40 C.F.R. pts. 122 and 412). EPA contracts 15 with DNR to administer and enforce the NPDES program. Under 16 the NPDES program, AFOs are referred to as concentrated animal 17 feeding operations (CAFOs). The purpose of both state and 18 federal regulation is to prevent manure or effluent from 19 contaminating groundwater and surface water. In compliance 20 with federal law, manure from a CAFO cannot be disposed of 21 or discharged in a manner that will cause surface water or 22 groundwater pollution (Code section 459.311). However, in all 23 cases involving the disposal of manure and effluent originating 24 from an AFO, rules adopted by the EPC can be no more stringent 25 than federal law (Code sections 455B.173, 459.311, and 26 459A.401). 27 BILLâS PROVISIONS.
